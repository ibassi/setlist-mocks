Catchy — privacy

The short version: your audio is matched and thrown away, your data stays on your phone, and there are no accounts.

What stays on your phone

  • Your sets, catches, timelines, and library — stored locally, never uploaded to us. We run no servers and hold no user data.
  • Short audio clips (~20 seconds) are kept only for moments the app couldn't identify, so you can listen and decide later. They live on your device, auto-delete on a schedule you control, and never leave.

What never exists

  • No recordings of your night. Audio is fingerprinted for song recognition and discarded on the spot.
  • No account, no sign-up, no email, no tracking, no analytics SDKs, no ads.

What leaves your phone, and when

  • Short audio fingerprints go to Apple's song-recognition service (ShazamKit) to identify tracks — the same way the Shazam app works. They identify songs, not you.
  • If you connect Spotify, the app talks to Spotify directly from your phone to create your playlists. Your Spotify tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain; we never see them.
  • Track titles are looked up against Apple's public iTunes catalog for artwork and previews.
